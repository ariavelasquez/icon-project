Firearms to Fast Cash: How Gun Buybacks Work

It's no secret that New York City has seen a spike in gun violence this year. Shootings across all five boroughs are up year-over-year with devastating consequences. One solution authorities have used to try to decrease the number of firearms on the streets is gun buybacks.

⚠️ Important Note: While shootings in NYC are up year-over-year, virtually every corner of the city is down from 10-year highs and all-time highs in the 90s.

NYPD and the borough District Attorney decide to host an event on neutral ground. (Think: a church, a community center, etc.)

The event begins and people show up with their firearms in a paper bag, a plastic bin, or a locked box.

The weapon gets turned over to law enforcement officers present in the building.

Officers check to make sure the gun isn't loaded. If it is loaded, they empty it. Once it's confirmed that all bullets have been removed, it's put away.

The person who brought the weapon receives their money in the form of a gift card (up to $200/gun for a maximum of 3 guns at $600).

After the event, all of the firearms collected are taken to a secure location and melted down/destroyed.
